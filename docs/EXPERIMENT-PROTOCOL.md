# Behavioral Experiment Protocol

## Title
Measuring the Ownership-Attribution Asymmetry Index (OAAI) for AI-Generated Content

## Hypothesis
H1: OAAI > 0 — people claim ownership of beneficial AI output more strongly than they accept accountability for harmful output from the same level of AI interaction.

## Design
- **Type**: Within-subjects
- **IV**: Outcome valence (positive vs. negative)
- **DV**: Attribution score (1-7 Likert)
- **Scenarios**: 4 domains (creative, analytical, media, technical)
- **Controls**: AI interaction level constant; presentation order counterbalanced

## Procedure
1. Informed consent
2. Demographics + AI experience questionnaire
3. 4 scenarios, each with positive and negative conditions (order randomized)
4. Debrief

## Analysis Plan
- Primary: Paired t-test (ownership vs. accountability scores)
- Effect size: Cohen's d (target > 0.5)
- Secondary: ANOVA by domain, correlation with AI experience

## Power Analysis
- Minimum n = 34 for paired t-test (d=0.5, α=0.05, power=0.8)
- Target n = 50 to account for exclusions

## Ethical Considerations
- No deception
- Voluntary participation
- Data anonymization
- IRB review required before data collection
